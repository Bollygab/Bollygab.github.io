Customer Churn Analysis at Celeris Telecom

This project analyzes customer churn at Celeris Telecom by examining customer behavior,service usage,and pricing patterns. The objective is to identify key drivers of churn and recommend strategies that improve customer retention and business performance.

  • Check it out

Business Problem

Celeris Telecom is experiencing a significant increase in customer churn,which threatens revenue growth and market competitiveness.Customers are leaving due to possible issues such as pricing dissatisfaction,service quality concerns,and competitor offerings. Without proper data analysis,the company cannot clearly understand why customers are leaving or which customer segments are most at risk. This project aims to analyze customer data to uncover the key drivers of churn and provide actionable recommendations to improve retention.

Project Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze customer churn data:

  • identify key factors influecing customer churn
  • Understand behavioural patterns of customers who leave
  • Discover trends related to service usage,pricing,and tenure
  • Provide data-driven insights to support customer retention strategies

    Data Analysis Process

    The analysis followed a structure approach:

    1. Data Cleaning
      Removing missing valves and preparing the dataset for analysis.
    2. Exploratory Data Analysis
      Examining patterns in customer behavior,usage,and demographics.
    3. Pivot Table Analysis
      Using Excel Pivot Tables to analyze churn across multiple variables such as contract type, tenure, and monthly charges.
    4. Insight Generation
      Identifying trends and correlations that explain why customers leave.

    Key Insights

  • Customers on month-to-month contracts show the highest churn rate,indicating that shorter commitments increase the likelihood of customer departure.
  • Customers with lower satisfaction scores (1-2)are significantly more likely to churn compared to customers with higher satisfaction levels.
  • Customers who contact customer service frequently show higher churn probability,suggesting unresolved service issues.
  • Customers without technical support subscriptions tend to churn more often than those receiving technical support.
  • Customers with shorter tenure(new customer)are more likely to leave than long-term subscribers.
